Exploring the World of Literature

Classic novels have had a profound impact on literature, shaping the way stories are told and influencing countless generations of writers. Works like Don Quixote by Miguel de Cervantes and Jane Eyre by Charlotte Brontë introduced complex characters and themes that explored the human condition in groundbreaking ways. The novel Moby-Dick by Herman Melville delved into the struggle between man and nature, while 1984 by George Orwell raised important questions about the dangers of totalitarianism. These books have become timeless, not only because of their narrative innovation but also for their deep exploration of identity, morality, and social structures, which continue to resonate with readers across time and culture.
